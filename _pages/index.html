Event Overview

Civic Spark is a gathering for people who care about the future of public life.

We're bringing together organizers, technologists, public servants, mutual aid networks, researchers, designers, and community leaders from across North America.

This isn’t a pitch stage or a showcase. It’s a space to share real stories, challenge assumptions, and explore what works — and what doesn’t — when building for civic impact.

Whether you’re deep in the work or just getting started, you’re invited.

Why Now

Today's challenges are big, and the work can feel fragmented, exhausting, and unclear. Civic Spark is a chance to reconnect — with purpose, with people, and with the possibilities of what we can build together.

  • Let’s share what’s working — and name what’s not.
  • Let’s build across disciplines, roles, and lived experience.
  • Let’s co-create what’s next.

We believe civic infrastructure can be resilient, public trust can be restored, and those most impacted can lead the way forward.

Who Should Attend

If you're building something better — whether that’s a community, a project, a policy, or a platform — Civic Spark is for you.

Maybe you want to be a:

  • public servant working collaboratively with communities.
  • grassroots organizer exploring tech or data as a tool for justice.
  • mutual aid volunteer making things work with what you’ve got.
  • designer, technologist, or researcher committed to public interest work.
  • student or newcomer looking to learn, connect, and contribute.

Or someone who's just figuring out where you fit — but knows things need to change — whether formally or informally, institutionally or on the ground — you are invited.
